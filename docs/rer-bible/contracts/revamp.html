Contracts revamp

Complete revamp of the contracts system. The three types there are currently will be removed from the mod and will leave their place to 1 simpler and unified mechanism.
This system will offer a way to players to experience the life of a monster hunter without the need to leave themselves to the mercy of a randomly generated quest.

I will start by showing what the mod menu will look like, this will demonstrate the whole setup and what to expect from it. Then i will go in details in how each step will work.

The contracts mod menu

  • Real life hours before new contracts. Default: 1.
  • Number of nearby easy contract. Default: 1
  • Number of nearby hard contract. Default: 1
  • Number of far easy contract. Default: 1
  • Number of far hard contract. Default: 1
  • Allow RER to learn about new locations as you explore the world. Default: true
  • Contracts generation seed. Default: 0
  • Chances for:
    • - Ogroid contracts. Default: 10
    • - Draconid contracts. Default: 10
    • - Specter contracts. Default: 10
    • - Necrophage contracts. Default: 10
    • - Elemental contracts. Default: 10

Starting a contract

RER now displays only one (1) errand on the noticeboard. This errand, once picked, will tell the mod to display a dialogue menu with different options. These options will depend on the values you set in the menu.

  • First it will display the list of nearby easy contracts for a random monster species you can accept.
  • In second, the list of nearby hard contracts
  • In third, the list of far easy contracts
  • In third, the list of far hard contracts

At this moment you can only accept one of the given contracts, to start a second one you will have to go back to the noticeboard and take the RER errand again. Doing so will cancel any currently unfinished RER contract you have in progress.

Once you picked the contract of your choice, RER will then pick among the list of known and discovered locations in the world based on the distance of the contract. Reach the location and the contract will start.

Known locations and discovered locations

RER comes with a pre-defined list of known locations, these locations are hand-picked by the contributors and make for interesting places for contracts.

However RER has the ability to learn about new locations as you explore the world. If the option is enabled, every time you will slay an enemy the mod will be notified and it will scan the surrounding for new potential locations.

When a contract is picked, the mod will then have the ability to pick locations from the hand-made locations but also the discovered locations for a greater variety.

The hunting phase

Once the location reached and depending on the disance and the difficulty of the contract, you will face different kinds of situations:

  • Monsters nests
  • Hordes of monsters
  • A boss fight

The objective is never really far away from the location of the contract, but will often require some searching to find it.

The distance & difficulty of contracts

The distance of a contract decides if the picked location you have to reach will be around the noticeboard or if it will be around a neighbooring settlement or isle.

The difficulty of the contract decides the risks to encounter a challenging creature during the completion of the encounter.

Completing contracts and rewards

Every time you will complete a contract, you will earn a token of gratitude from the locals that you will be able to trade for valuables to the bounty master. For each token the trader will offer various types of rewards:

  • Consumables
  • Crafting materials
  • Gear
  • Golds
  • Experience

The rewards token you get from the completion of the contract however depends on the location where you started the contract. A poor Velen settlement does not have the same rewards as Novigrad for example.

Waiting for new contracts

Each noticeboard has a limit of contracts you can complete in a given lapse of time. The length depends on the value you set in the menu, and it is expressed in real life hours. That means meditating won't cause you to get new contracts and the only way to get new ones is to go for another noticeboard.

The contracts that are generated each interval are seeded from the time spent in the playthrough and the location. This means that during the first hour of a playthrough the contract in White Orchard will be the same for all players across all computers.

If needed, the mod menu exposes a slider that can be changed to use another seed. However this will follow the same logic, any player with the same seed will get the same contracts or the same amount of time spent in the playthrough.
